How to cook Chicken Biriyani.

What is Chicken Biriyani

Biryani is a celebratory rice and meat dish cherished in the Indian sub-continent.
A traditional biryani consists of fluffy basmati rice layered over tender & succulent pieces of meat, accompanied with the mesmerizing aromas of spices, herbs & caramelized onions.

ingredients needed :

  • 3 large red onions,sliced thin
  • 200ml of peanut oil for frying
  • 1kg chicken thighs,skin off
  • 4 tbsp yogurt
  • 1 tsp chilli powder
  • 1/2 tsp ground tumeric
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 3 tsp ginger paste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Fried Onions:
    Heat the peanut oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
    Add the thinly sliced onions in batches and fry until golden brown and crispy (8–10 minutes per batch).
    Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
    Set aside half of the fried onions for garnishing.
  2. Marinate the Chicken:
    In a large bowl, mix yoghurt, chilli powder, turmeric, garam masala, ginger paste, garlic paste, salt, and lemon juice.
    Add the chicken thighs and mix well to coat.
    Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour (or overnight for best results).
  3. Cook the Rice:
    In a large pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil.
    Add the soaked rice, cardamom pods, bay leaf, cloves, cinnamon stick, and salt.
    Cook the rice until it’s 70% cooked (slightly undercooked).
    Drain and set aside.
  4. Cook the Chicken: Heat 3-4 tbsp of the leftover oil (from frying onions) in a large pot or deep skillet over medium heat.
    Add the marinated chicken and cook for 10–12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is almost cooked through and the marinade thickens.
    Remove from heat.
  5. Layer the Biryani:
    In a large pot or deep pan, spread a layer of the cooked chicken at the bottom.
    Add half of the rice as the next layer, followed by a sprinkle of fried onions, chopped coriander, and mint leaves.
    Add the remaining rice as the top layer.
    Drizzle with the saffron milk (if using), melted ghee, and a final sprinkle of fried onions.
  6. Cook the Biryani (Dum Cooking):
    Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid.
    If the lid is loose, seal it with a piece of foil or dough.
    Cook on very low heat for 20–25 minutes.
    This allows the flavors to meld together.
  7. Serve:
    Gently mix the layers before serving to distribute the flavors evenly.
    Serve hot with raita, salad, or pickle.
